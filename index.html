About Me

I am a native of Atlanta, Georgia. I have a B.S. in Financial Management from Clemson University and an MBA from Arizona State University. I enjoy spending my free time visiting friends and family, watching sports, working out, and occasionally officiating a wedding. My passion for technology and prior professional experience has led me to the Georgia Tech Bootcamp for full stack web development based on its prior success and formidable reputation.

Atlanta has become a technological playground for a number of large companies, both domestic and international. My goal after completing the bootcamp is to connect with key stakeholders within those companies seeking coding talent. Specifically, I will seek to learn how these companies utilize their developers to make a profit and how I can contribute in that forum. I would also like to explore the long-term career paths that exist after beginning in a developer role with a company. Contacts I have made in the field of coding thus far have emphasized a need for individuals that can understand the principals and theoretical approach to software development and translate the feasibiltiy of project accomplishment to Senior Management. Conversely, those same individuals expressed a desire to understand the needs of Senior Management as it relates to increasing profits with respect to internal and external programming deliverables. I believe my professional experience can provide assistance in bridging that gap within a company after completion of this bootcamp.
